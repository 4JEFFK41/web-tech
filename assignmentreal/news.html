  • Latest update - 30/05/2020

    UK Government give the green light to resumption of competitive sport behind closed doors. Providing all conditions are met, one of the first major sporting events is expected to be the 2000 Guineas Stakes race meeting. Following detailed discussions with the Deputy Chief Medical Officers and Public Health England, preparations are also being finalised for the Premier League and EFL football to resume later in June.

  • Update 2 - 29/05/2020

    Groups of up to six people will be able to meet in England from Monday 1st June, including in gardens and other private outdoors spaces, provided strict social distancing guidelines are followed. The Prime Minister announced the change as he set out a carefully-designed package to ease the burdens of lockdown in a way that is expected to keep the R rate down.

  • Update 3 - 24/05/2020

    Schools, colleges and nurseries are set to re-open in phasesfrom the 1st June, The Prime Minister said pupils in reception, Year 1 and Year 6 will be the first to go back from the start of next month as part of a staged process. Nurseries would also be covered in the initial phase and the hope is that all primary school children would return to class by the summer. Secondary schools are looking reopen after September.

  • Update 4 - 21/05/2020

    Trial of the rapid coronavirus test launched in Hampshire. The new test, which has been proven effective in clinical settings, does not need to be sent to a laboratory to be processed and can return results within 20 minutes. The test will be rolled out to a number of A&E departments without access to laboratories, GP coronavirus testing hubs and care homes across Hampshire.
